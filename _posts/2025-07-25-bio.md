### Pukyong National University Research Published in **Top 0.12% Global Journal**

{%

  image="images/top12full.jpg"
%}

A research team from **Pukyong National University (President Bae Sang-Hoon)** has published a landmark review article on **biomimetic nanomaterial platforms for laser-based cancer diagnosis and therapy** in _Coordination Chemistry Reviews_, a globally renowned Elsevier journal with an **Impact Factor of 23.5** and ranked in the **top 0.12%** of JCR journals.

The article, titled
**“Functional Nanostructured Biomaterials in Cancer Phototherapy and Biomedicine,”**
investigates how advanced nanoplatforms inspired by biological systems significantly enhance the precision, safety, and effectiveness of **photothermal therapy (PTT)** and **photodynamic therapy (PDT)** for real-world clinical cancer treatments.

#### Overview of the Research

The study provides an in-depth discussion on:

- Design principles of functional nanoplatforms
- Surface modification and functionalization strategies
- Biomedical integration and clinical translation potential

It highlights emerging developments in **organic, inorganic, and polymer-based nanomaterials**, emphasizing how these systems improve:

- **Biocompatibility**
- **Targeting efficiency**
- **Degradation control**

These advances address long-standing challenges of conventional nanomedicine such as cytotoxicity and low tumor specificity, ensuring safer and more effective applications in real clinical environments.

---

#### Research Significance

The paper has gained recognition for providing **critical insights into next-generation precision medicine**. It proposes innovative strategies for designing laser-responsive nano photosensitizers capable of enhancing diagnostic accuracy and therapeutic performance in cancer treatment.

This research underscores the transformative role of nanotechnology in future **smart healthcare and advanced photomedicine systems**.
